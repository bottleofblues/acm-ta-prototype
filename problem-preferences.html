Assessment of Problem Preferences

Based on Michael Watkins’ The First 90 Days. This exercise helps you clarify which kinds of problems you naturally like to solve — and where you may need to stretch or build complementary support.

Part 1 — Interest Ratings by Domain

Rate your intrinsic interest in solving problems in each domain on a scale of 1–10, where 1 = very little interest and 10 = a great deal of interest.

Part 2 — Problem Preference Matrix

Your ratings are mapped into a matrix of functions (rows) and problem types (columns). This helps reveal where you naturally lean — technically, politically, or culturally.

Reflection

Reflect on the patterns you see in your matrix and ratings: Where do you most enjoy solving problems? Where are you least eager? What vulnerabilities might that create in your new role?

Optional: Ask ACM TA for Insight

Once you’ve completed the ratings and matrix, you can ask the ACM TA for a short coaching read-out.
